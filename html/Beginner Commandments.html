Beginner Commandments

"Night gathers, and now my learning begins. It shall not end until my death. I shall take no breaks, hold no hands, father no children. I shall wear no crowns and with no glory. I shall learn and code at my computer. I am the sword on the internet. I am the watcher of code. I am the shield that guards the realms of java. I pledge my life and honor to the Learn Java Project, for this night and all the nights to come."

The Learn Java Project oath (adapted from Game of Thrones - Night's Watch)

Getting started

  • Assumption: you have no experience programming
  • Expectation: By the end of part 1, you should have a fairly basic knowledge of programming
  • Expectation: By the end of part 2, you should have a fairly basic knowledge of how to build classes, build an application

Guidelines

  1. Practice by doing.It's similar to how you would learn a real language. It won't help to bury your nose in a "How to Speak French" book. You would learn by reading then by speaking. So go CODE!
  2. Learn the fundamentals and find a way to apply it in a way that interests you. This can be an effective way to learn.
  3. Get Help. Open your ♥ to the community. There are so many people willing to help. Get help!
